This site is all about ad-hoc sharing

Just use the #need or #offer hash tags in tweets to make contact with someone else nearby. For instance, post a tweet like :

Does anyone have digging #tools? I could do with some help at 23 Avonside Drive. #need #eqnz

..and it will show up on the page of requests for help in linwood.

The keys to making it work are:

  1. #need - made it show up under requests for help. Or, use #offer to tag offers.
  2. "at 23 Avonside Drive" - made it show up under linwood
  3. #eqnz or #chch - was needed to make it appear in the feed at all

If you put your address (or suburb) just be sure to put it after the words "at .." or "in ..". That way this system will automatically 'geocode' it to the right place.

This is not an official govt site, nor does it claim to be one. Nor does this claim to be the one and only place to go for any of this stuff.

Places like http://eq.org.nz are probably the best place for official, verifiable information. Other sites might be the go if you need specific crisis help right now.

By contrast, this site is about the ad-hoc/spontaneous, 'crowdsourced' collaboration thing. Make up your own #hashtags for whatever purpose you need. If you send out a tweet maybe one of your friends will see it first, or maybe a neighbour will make contact via this site, or maybe - just maybe - someone from the Student Volunteer Army will see it as well? (Well, I certainly can't make any claims or promises for them, you best go fill out their form as well).

The thing is, just tweet things out in the normal way - but be sure to include an #offer, #helpme or #need hashtag when you do so. Hey, it can't hurt!

Add your own!

  1. Tweet out your #offer or #need
  2. Include your location
  3. Include the hashtag #chch or #eqnz
